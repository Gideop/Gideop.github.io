Mar 2022 - Present
* Lead Engineer, Future Hypersonics Study, AFLCMC/EBX, Eglin AFB, FL

The Future Hypersonics Study is focused on various high-priority studies to help answer Air Force Senior Leadership's most pressing questions and concerns in the area of hypersonics. A primary objective is building a cloneable digital acquisition construct for future hypersonic weapons. Under my leadership and working with the Armament Directorate's Digital Acquisition and Sustainment Office (DASO), the EBX Future Hypersonic Study is identifying and populating the attributes of a desired weapon system into a reference architecture, built in Cameo and using SySML. The reference architecture guides and constrains the Contractor's Implementation Model (CIM) to focus on aspects that uniquely add value to the Government, whether it be modularity, commonality, or particular performance metrics. The reference architecture also forms the basis of a digital evaluation environment for rapid evaluation of a digital proposal, as captured by the Contractor's CIM. Partnering with 10 external organizations, including Industry, FFRDCs, UARCs, and National Labs, the reference architecture is being developed in a collaborative environment, within a unified IDE implemented on S-FENCES.

Led the development of an Agile Systems Engineering strategy that created a spectrum of requirements from Minimum Military Utility to full End-State capability, requiring Prime Contractors to commit to a capability, through a derived System Performance Specification, falling within that spectrum that must be verified in a fast-paced, extremely Agile, competitive MTA Rapid Prototyping effort. The result is a flexible construct allowing rapid vetting and negotiation of System Requirements, providing the Government with the necessary tools to maximize performance, while minimizing cost and schedule risk.
